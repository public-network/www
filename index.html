Public Network

The interstate of humanity.

Who you are. What you own. What you say. What you exchange. What you authorize. These five categories of fact underpin any functioning society, and today most of these records are controlled by a small number of private companies and institutions. They live on servers and in registries that can change the rules, restrict access, or remove records at any time.

Public Network is a protocol that records these five facts onchain: identity, asset, information, transaction, and authentication. It is an open set of schemas that anyone can read from and write to. No participant can modify, remove, or gate them. Anyone can build on top; the protocol itself holds no position on what gets built.

The minimalism is deliberate. A public good must be small enough to audit, simple enough to verify, and narrow enough to stay neutral. Applications read and write the five primitives through an open source SDK. The protocol itself is credibly neutral, permanent, and built onchain.

Public Network is secured by Ethereum. It does not run its own chain and does not operate its own consensus. Transactions settle in Units, the protocol's stable unit of account. Units is a neutral means of exchange, fully backed by reserves held transparently onchain, and issued by no single jurisdiction. Units is price-stable by design and has no speculation mechanism; it exists only to denominate value within the protocol. The protocol fee flows to a treasury stewarded by Patrons, with progressive decentralization over time. Anyone can verify protocol state directly, with or without a Public Network application.

Identity

Who they are.

A record of an entity that holds a cryptographic key, such as an individual, a group, an institution, or an autonomous agent. Today, identity is rented from platforms. A social account, an email login, a government ID, and so on. Each can be revoked, suspended, or deleted. On Public Network, an identity is bound to the keys that created it and works across every application on the protocol. It cannot be taken away. Identities may be pseudonymous; the protocol binds keys, not names.

Identities are ERC-721 tokens (PNI). Each identity owns attributes such as a primary handle, a set of linked wallets, and an avatar. External accounts from social, professional, and communication platforms may be linked to extend reputation across systems. These links are additive and revocable; the identity functions with none attached.

Asset

What they own.

A record of ownership over a resource, such as property, art, data, or intellectual work. Value in these resources depends on provable origin, yet most assets today carry no such record. On Public Network, every asset is attested at creation and every transfer is recorded.

Assets are ERC-721 tokens (PNA) that compose. A video work with a projector and crate. A remix and its source tracks. A dataset and its sources. Each relationship is recorded as a composition edge, and royalty paths are recorded through the graph to upstream contributors via ERC-2981. The full lineage of any work, including its origin, contributors, and components, is readable without permission.

Information

What they say.

A record of content signed by an identity, such as a message, a post, an article, a claim, or a piece of research. Where it is published today determines whether it survives, whether it can be found, and whether it can be attributed. When the platform disappears or changes policy, the record is lost. On Public Network, information carries a cryptographic signature, a timestamp, and an immutable reference to its author.

Information lives inside Spaces (PNS), addressable containers for threads, publications, communities, and more. Anyone can create a Space.

Transaction

What they did.

A record of value exchange between identities, such as a sale, a listing, a coin issuance, or a cross-chain settlement. The digital economy today runs through intermediaries that take twenty to thirty percent of every sale and can deny service at any time. On Public Network, transactions are recorded at the protocol layer. Each transaction carries the signatures of its participants; fees route at the protocol layer.

The protocol fee is fixed at 1% of transaction value. Hard-coded, non-configurable. Fees settle in Units alongside the transaction itself; the fee flows to the Public Network treasury. Applications may layer additional fees; the originator retains the remainder.

Authentication

What they authorized.

A record of an authorized action, such as a sign-in, a delegation to an agent, or an approval of a third-party application. Most authentication today runs through servers owned by a handful of companies that control which applications can access which users. On Public Network, an identity signs every authorization directly. Every action on the protocol carries the signature of the identity behind it, permanently and publicly.

An identity may delegate scoped, time-bound authority to another key and revoke it at any time. Every delegation and revocation is recorded; the chain of authority is always walkable.

Over time, these signatures accumulate into an identity's reputation: a permanent, public record of its actions and what has been attested about it. Applications and readers choose whom to trust through this record. No protocol-level gatekeeper decides who is credible.

Applications

What they build.

Applications are built on the protocol. Anyone may build one: an individual, a group, an institution, or an autonomous agent. No permission is granted because none is required. A marketplace, an open source model, a credential system, a provenance registry, a self-governing collective. Each reads and writes the same five primitives. None owns the substrate. None can remove another. Nothing built on it can be captured. A user who leaves one application, or who builds their own, carries their identity, their assets, their history, and their authority with them. Those records live in the protocol. Applications read and write them, but never hold them.

Interface

How they see.

Applications reach people through interfaces. A canonical open source interface lives at public.network: a reference implementation over every pillar of the protocol, and not the only one. Anyone may fork it, adapt it, embed it, or replace it entirely. Every interface inherits the same substrate: the identity, the assets, the information, the transactions, and the authority recorded onchain. The protocol is infrastructure. The interface is how it is seen.

Patrons

Decentralize and open source humanity.

Patrons protect, support, and evolve Public Network one initiative at a time. They steward the Public Network treasury, which funds audits, infrastructure, and ecosystem grants. Any individual, group, institution, or autonomous agent can become a Patron by contributing to Public Network.

The Perfection of the Imperfect Circle

Feeling insecure and alienated from others, the Imperfect Circle tried to join the ranks of the perfect circles, but over time its true self started to diminish along with the traces of its imperfect shape.

After the long quest for an ideally manufactured line, the Imperfect Circle realized that perfection was an abstraction and utopia, an elusive ideal with many interpretations that was impossible to achieve. Eventually, the purest form of perfection was found in its stead: authenticity with all its flaws and mistakes. The Imperfect Circle came back to its innermost being, its original form, unique, real, and free, accepting the innate beauty of itself and others.

But its imperfect shape can only be seen and appreciated by those who are able to look deeper and recognize its true form.
